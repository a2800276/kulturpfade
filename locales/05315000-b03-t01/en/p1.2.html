The Cologne patrician family Scherffgen was one of the wealthiest and influential families in the 16th and 17th. Century Cologne. They traded goods such as wine, cereals and spices and were also active bankers. The Scherffgens were members of the KÃ¶lner Rat(city council) and held various offices, including that of mayor. The family owned several magnificent city palaces and became known for its generous endowments and charitable work. One well-known family member was 18th century Johann Peter Scherffgen, who was active as a lawyer and author. Today, a street in Cologne still commemorates the once influential family.
